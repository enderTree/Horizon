## [Entire Herculaneum Scroll Read Using AI for the First Time](https://scrollprize.org/firstscroll) ⭐️ 9.0/10

The Vesuvius Challenge team has successfully read an entire Herculaneum scroll (PHerc. 1667) end-to-end using AI and advanced imaging techniques, unlocking ancient Greek philosophical texts that have been unreadable for nearly 2,000 years. This breakthrough demonstrates that AI can non-destructively recover lost classical works from carbonized scrolls, potentially transforming archaeology and classical scholarship. It raises hope that many more lost texts from the only surviving library of antiquity could be recovered. The scroll, PHerc. 1667, was virtually unwrapped and read without physically opening the fragile carbonized roll. The team used high-resolution CT scans and machine learning models to detect ink and segment the surface, publishing their methods and results in a preprint and on GitHub.

hackernews · verditelabs · Jun 25, 15:48 · [Discussion](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48675179)

**Background**: The Herculaneum papyri are over 1,800 scrolls carbonized by the eruption of Mount Vesuvius in AD 79. They were discovered in the 18th century at the Villa of the Papyri and contain Greek philosophical texts, including many works by the Epicurean philosopher Philodemus. Traditional attempts to unroll them caused damage; the Vesuvius Challenge, launched in 2023, offered prizes to teams using AI and computer vision to read them without physical contact.

## [German Court Holds Google Liable for AI Overview Errors](https://simonwillison.net/2026/Jun/25/ai-and-liability/#atom-everything) ⭐️ 9.0/10

A German regional court ruled that Google is directly liable for false statements generated by its AI Overviews, rejecting defenses that users should verify information themselves. This landmark ruling sets a precedent that AI agents are treated as agents of their deployers, meaning companies cannot hide behind faulty AI to avoid liability. It could reshape liability standards for AI-generated content globally. The court held that AI Overviews are not neutral summaries but expressions of Google's own words, and that previous limited liability protections for search engines do not apply. Google has announced it will appeal the decision.

rss · Simon Willison · Jun 25, 22:28

**Background**: AI Overviews are summaries generated by large language models that appear at the top of search results. Traditionally, search engines were not liable for snippets from third-party sites, but the German court distinguished AI-generated content as originating from the company itself. Bruce Schneier argues that treating AI as agents of deployers prevents companies from gaining an unfair advantage by using cheaper, error-prone AI while escaping responsibility.

## [Age verification laws threaten online privacy, warn critics](https://expression.fire.org/p/the-papers-please-era-of-the-internet) ⭐️ 8.0/10

An article warns that government-mandated age verification and digital ID laws are expanding a surveillance infrastructure that destroys online privacy, forcing users to submit sensitive documents for everyday internet access. If adopted without privacy safeguards, these laws could normalize constant identity checks, chilling free expression and enabling mass surveillance. Cryptographic solutions like anonymous credentials offer a way to verify age without revealing identity, but their adoption faces political and technical barriers. The article points to laws like the UK Online Safety Act that require platforms to verify user ages. It discusses technical solutions such as anonymous credentials and zero-knowledge proofs, which can prove attributes like 'over 18' without revealing actual identity or linking requests.

hackernews · bilsbie · Jun 25, 21:44 · [Discussion](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48679608)

**Background**: Digital credentials are electronic equivalents of physical IDs like passports or driver's licenses. Anonymous credential systems, a type of attribute-based credential, allow a user to prove certain properties (e.g., age over 18) without revealing their identity, using cryptographic protocols. Zero-knowledge proofs enable one party to convince another that a statement is true without conveying any additional information beyond the statement's truth. These technologies could allow age verification without sacrificing privacy, but they are not yet widely deployed.

## [Zig's new bitCast semantics and LLVM backend improvements](https://ziglang.org/devlog/2026/#2026-06-25) ⭐️ 8.0/10

Zig's devlog dated June 25, 2026 introduces new @bitCast semantics that are endian-agnostic and target-independent, alongside LLVM backend improvements for arbitrary-width integers and bit-packed data. This change simplifies bit-level operations in Zig, making it easier to work with binary protocols and bit-packed structures without manual bit shuffling, and improves performance by leveraging LLVM optimizations. Under the new semantics, @bitCast operates on logical bit representation where the first array element becomes the least significant bits, effectively adopting a little-endian convention. The LLVM backend improvements enable better code generation for non-standard integer widths.

**Background**: Zig is a systems programming language prioritizing safety and performance. @bitCast is a built-in function that reinterprets the bits of a value as a different type without conversion. Arbitrary-width integers allow manipulation of integers with any bit width, not limited to powers of two, which is useful for bit-packed data where values use non-standard bit widths to save space.

This shift represents a major infrastructure change for AI and computing, as datacenter operators increasingly bypass public grids to secure reliable power, impacting energy markets and grid planning nationwide. Behind-the-meter datacenters are directly powered by on-site generation (often gas-fired) rather than relying solely on the utility grid; OpenAI's Stargate initiative is a key driver, with plans to commit 10 GW of capacity by end of 2025, mostly behind-the-meter.

rss · Semianalysis · Jun 25, 19:48

**Background**: Behind-the-meter (BTM) power refers to electricity generation that is connected on the customer's side of the utility meter, allowing facilities like datacenters to operate independently from the grid. Grid constraints such as long interconnection queues, limited transmission capacity, and regulatory bottlenecks have made it difficult for new datacenters to connect to the grid in time. As a result, developers are increasingly turning to modular, BTM generation solutions—including natural gas turbines and battery storage—to power their facilities.

## [UN Adopts First Global Technical Regulation for Autonomous Driving](https://global.chinadaily.com.cn/a/202606/25/WS6a3cf7e7a310986e2b461ebc.html) ⭐️ 8.0/10

The UN World Forum for Harmonization of Vehicle Regulations (WP.29) has adopted the first global technical regulation for Automated Driving Systems (ADS GTR), co-led by China, the United States, the European Union, the United Kingdom, Canada, and Japan. This regulation establishes the first internationally harmonized safety and lifecycle requirements for autonomous vehicles, setting a global baseline that major economies will incorporate into their national laws, thereby accelerating the deployment of safe autonomous driving technology. The regulation covers product core technical indicators, manufacturer safety management, safety archives, testing and validation, and post-deployment safety, forming a full lifecycle framework. China contributed technical proposals and test data, and its national mandatory standard aligns with the ADS GTR, with further details for L3 and L4 levels.

telegram · zaihuapd · Jun 25, 16:03

**Background**: The regulation was developed under the UN 1998 Agreement on Global Technical Regulations, within the Working Party on Automated/Autonomous and Connected Vehicles (GRVA) of WP.29. After approval by GRVA in January 2026, the draft GTR was submitted to WP.29 for adoption. It is expected that each contracting party will initiate processes to incorporate the GTR into their national regulatory systems.
